Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 28, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Spendly collects, uses, and protects information when you use our personal expense tracking application. By using Spendly, you agree to the practices described below.

1. Data We Collect

When you create an account, we collect basic information you provide directly, such as your name, email address, and password (stored in a hashed form, never in plain text).

As you use Spendly, we store the financial data you choose to enter — including expense amounts, categories, dates, and descriptions — so that we can display your transaction history and summaries back to you.

We also collect limited, automatic technical information such as your IP address, browser type, and timestamps of access, used to keep the service secure and diagnose issues.

2. How We Use Your Data

We use the information we collect solely to operate Spendly and improve your experience. This includes authenticating your account, displaying your expense records, generating summaries and category breakdowns you request, and applying filters and search.

We do not use your data for advertising, marketing campaigns, or to build advertising profiles. We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information or expense data to third parties.

3. Data Storage and Security

Your data is stored on secure servers and protected with industry-standard safeguards, including encryption of passwords and access controls limited to authorized personnel. We take reasonable steps to prevent unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction of your information.

Despite these measures, no system can be completely secure. You use Spendly at your own risk and are responsible for keeping your login credentials confidential. If you believe your account has been compromised, please contact us immediately.

We retain your data for as long as your account is active. When you close your account, your data is removed from active systems within a reasonable period in accordance with our retention practices, except where a longer retention is required by law.

4. Third-Party Services

Spendly uses a small number of third-party services to operate — for example, hosting infrastructure and email delivery for account-related messages. These providers have access only to the information needed to perform their service and are contractually obligated to protect it.

We do not embed third-party advertising networks or analytics that track you across other websites. If we ever add optional integrations (such as importing transactions from your bank), we will update this policy and ask for your consent before enabling them.

5. Your Rights and Choices

You can access, update, correct, or delete your personal information at any time through your account settings or by contacting us. You may also export a copy of your expense data in a portable format.

If you wish to close your account, we will remove your data from active systems as described above. We may retain limited records where required to comply with legal obligations or to resolve disputes.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in the service, legal requirements, or our practices. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page and, where appropriate, notify you through the service.

Your continued use of Spendly after any changes take effect constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy. If you do not agree to the updated policy, you should stop using the service and may close your account at any time.

7. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or how your data is handled, please reach out to us through the contact information provided on the Spendly project page.
